We Salute You: The Top 10 Housing Markets for Military and Veteran Homebuyers

By Julie Taylor
Jun 18, 2024
Envelope Facebook Twitter Linkedin Pinterest
Share

Americans in the military have a variety of government benefits available to them for their serviceand a new report reveals that certain cities excel at making veterans feel more at home.

To celebrate the 80-year anniversary of when the GI Bill of Rights was signed into law on June 22, Realtor.com has released a new report detailing the top housing markets tailored to military households in the U.S. To find these military-friendly metros, our economists sifted through America’s 100 largest metros analyzing the following factors:

  • Homeownership accessibility, estimated by the homeownership rate among military households
  • Veterans Affairs home loanuse, estimated by the number of sales financed by VA loans per 1,000 military households
  • Affordability, measured by the Realtor.com affordability score reflecting the share of home listings in a market that are affordable to homebuyers at various local income levels
  • Availability of homes for sale, measured by active listings per 1,000 existing households
  • Job stability, estimated by the forecasted unemployment rate

Taking the top spot is Des Moines. Iowa’s capital city boasts an 88.8% homeownership rate among military households, which is the highest rate of all top 10 markets. This market is followed by Augusta, GA, and Columbia, SC.

Here are the top military-friendly metros and what makes them unique.

Des Moines, IA

Surprisingly, the military homeownership rate in this metro is 0.18 percentage points higher than the nonmilitary counterparts.

“A higher homeownership rate among military households indicates greater accessibility to homeownership for these families,” says Realtor.com economist Jiayi Xu. “It also suggests high effectiveness of military benefits in promoting financial stability.”

Augusta, GA

Coming in No. 2 is Augusta-Richmond County, GA-SC. More people get VA loans here than in any other metro area in the top 10with an average of 27.8 home sales per 1,000 military households being financed by VA loans.

These loans enable qualified borrowers with smaller down payments, more flexible credit terms, and lower interest rates, which is especially attractive in today’s challenging market. It’s important to note that only 3 in 10 veterans know they can qualify for a loan without a down payment.

Columbia, SC

Nabbing the No. 3 spot on the list is Columbia, SC, thanks in part to its booming job market. Columbia has a forecasted unemployment rate of just 3.3%the lowest in the top 10.

“A lower forecasted unemployment rate suggests that one could encounter less competition when seeking employment and obtain better job security, a consideration for veterans and military households with workers who are not active military members,” Xu explains.

Birmingham-Hoover, AL

Ranking No. 4 is Birmingham-Hoover, AL, where homebuyers can get much more bang for their buck, according to theRealtor.com affordability score.

This score varies between 0 and 2, and a higher score indicates relatively high affordability.

With an affordability score of 0.83, Birmingham-Hoover tied with Indianapolis-Carmel-Anderson, IN, as the most affordable market within the top 10.

Palm Bay-Melbourne-Titusville, FL

No. 5 on the list is Palm Bay-Melbourne-Titusville, FL. This metro had the most active listings in the top 10 (65.8 per 1,000 existing households).

Xu says the availability of homes for sale is more important to home shoppers than ever due to the mortgage rate “lock-in” effectwhere homeowners with low-interest mortgages are hesitant to sell and buy new homes at a much higher interest rate.

“This has resulted in significantly lower for-sale inventory when compared to the pre-pandemic level,” Xu explains.

Rounding out the top 10 list are Little Rock-North Little Rock-Conway, AR; Indianapolis-Carmel-Anderson, IN; Memphis, TN-MS-AR; Richmond, VA; and San Antonio-New Braunfels, TX.
